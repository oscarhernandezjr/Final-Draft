About Me

Background

I am a US Army Ranger with a background in criminal justice and homeland security. Through past experiences, my objective became clear and I decided to make it my mission statement to combat child exploitation and supporting survivors through trauma-informed care and intervention strategies.

I am currently enrolled in the Washington Vets to Tech (WaV2T) program in the Cloud Application Development track, in where I would gain the necessary skills in languages such as HTML, Javascipt and Python.

I am driven to combine my criminal justice background with computer science to combat human trafficking and exploitation through an interdisciplinary method, aiming to make a meaningful impact in eradicating child trafficking and protecting vulnerable youth.

Interests

I must say, many of my interests helped me become the person I am today. I started playing soccer at a young age in my home country of Honduras. Soccer allowed me to meet many people from different countries, hence giving me the opportunity to see different cultures and perspectives.

I also enjoy mixed martial arts and practiced/coached for 10 years. Ironically, this also led me to travel and compete in Thailand, where I learned so much to what the world has to offer.
